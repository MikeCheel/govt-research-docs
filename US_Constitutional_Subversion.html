Wrote that federal government lacked constitutional authority for internal improvements, but political pressure led to inconsistent enforcement of this principle.

Over 6,000 Americans were impressed by British Navy before war - constitutional duty to protect citizens was neglected. [Miller Center - Madison]

Though called "Treasury notes," they circulated as de facto currency, violating Constitution's gold/silver requirement for states and federal monetary powers.
  • Sedition Act Prosecutions: Though he opposed Alien and Sedition Acts, failed to pardon all victims of Federalist prosecutions. Some journalists and critics remained imprisoned or under legal threat. As champion of First Amendment, should have immediately reversed all prior restraints on speech.
  • State Militia Federalization: During War of 1812, attempted to federalize state militias beyond constitutional authority. Governors of Connecticut and Massachusetts refused compliance, asserting state sovereignty. Madison's broad interpretation of war powers set precedent for future federal overreach of state military forces.
  • British Property Seizures: Authorized seizure of British property in US without due process during War of 1812. Violated Fifth Amendment taking clause by confiscating private property based solely on owner nationality. Property rights violated without individualized determination of threat.
  • Prisoner of War Policies: Authorized harsh treatment of British prisoners in retaliation for British policies. Violated international laws of war and constitutional protections. Some British prisoners held in conditions that violated human dignity and constitutional principles of proportionate punishment.
